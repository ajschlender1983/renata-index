Renata — Auto-Generated Broker Proposal (assessment + design)

Strategic-resolve session (benefits-broker SME, B2B proposal strategist, CTO/data, E&O counsel) on 2026-06-15, grounded in web research on industry-standard broker proposals. Question: can Renata auto-generate a professional-equivalent broker proposal from the data it already captures?

What an industry-standard broker proposal contains

A complete benefits-broker proposal runs ~10 sections: (1) client background & pain points, (2) broker firm overview, (3) account team, (4) services (renewal strategy, carrier negotiation, enrollment, ACA/ERISA/COBRA compliance, benchmarking), (5) technology/ben-admin, (6) compliance & fiduciary, (7) communication/education, (8) data analytics & cost control (the dimension where brokers differ most), (9) fees/compensation disclosure, (10) references/ case studies. Employers evaluate on ~8 areas and reward measurable differentiation and a side-by-side vs the incumbent, not generic service lists. Sources: Taylor Benefits RFP template; SHRM "Ultimate Guide to Hiring a Benefits Broker"; Griffin/HUB RFP-process guides.

The core reframe: two artifacts, not one

The SME and the CTO independently landed on the same split:

  • Artifact A — the prospecting "Switch Analysis" (pre-engagement). Its job is to win the meeting and the Agent-of-Record (AOR/BOR) letter, not to close on paper. Built from public 5500 + a one-time broker profile. No prospect-private data. This is the artifact that pries an account loose from an incumbent at renewal.
  • Artifact B — the marketed proposal (post-engagement). Firm carrier quotes, plan-design side-by-side, net-cost delta. Closes the account. Requires the broker to upload the prospect's plan summary/SBC, census, and the carrier quotes they get back.

A earns the meeting; the upload happens in/after it; B closes. Two stages of one funnel.

Efficacy verdict (strong yes, conditional)

A named, data-driven current-state diagnosis (their actual carrier, spend, cost-per-head, overpayment vs peers, renewal clock) materially out-converts a generic capabilities proposal. The mechanism is defeating status-quo bias (inertia, not the incumbent, is the real enemy), via a pattern interrupt ("how do they know this?") plus loss-aversion with a deadline. This is a diagnostic prospecting artifact (a personalized Challenger commercial insight), not an RFP-style proposal.

Structure for the win (invert the standard order): lead with the Diagnosis (the money page: carrier, spend, cost/head, peer benchmark, renewal clock, source footer), then the cost-and-window page, then the approach (a hypothesis, not a service menu), then compressed proof (firm + 2-3 relevant references), then one ask. Cut compliance/tech/comms detail to an appendix. Never put a fee schedule on a prospecting artifact (invites price comparison before there is a problem to solve).

Can Renata's data auto-generate a professional equivalent? YES, for Artifact A.

Field-level map of the 10 sections: - Sections 2,3,4,5,7,9,10 (firm, team, services, tech, comms, fees, references) = 100% broker profile, captured once at onboarding, reused on every proposal. - Section 1 (client background + pain) + the spend/benchmark half of Section 8 = 100% Renata data: companies.name/state/metro/naics/employee_band, plans.carrier/ incumbent_broker/total_premium/participants/cost_per_head/renewal_month, scores.reason_codes, peer_medians(), signals.type='rate_filing'. - Section 6 = profile + Renata's factual garnish (it read the prospect's own filing). - The only true gap is the back half of Section 8: plan design (deductibles/copays/ network/formulary), employee census (age/sex/dependents/zip), claims/loss-ratio, and live carrier quotes. None of these are in the 5500. And that gap is exactly what a prospecting proposal omits, so Artifact A populates ~9.5 of 10 sections.

The one make-or-break caveat

Renata's cost/head is a premium-per-head from public filings, not a risk-adjusted actuarial cost. A high number can be a deliberately richer plan, not waste. So Artifact A must say "you appear above the peer median on a premium basis; the plan-design comparison comes after we see your SBC," never "you overpay." That humility is what makes it read as a sharp analyst rather than a mail-merge, and it is the funnel hook that drives the upload unlocking Artifact B.

Guardrails (the discipline that is the whole ballgame)

  1. The savings-claim trap (efficacy + legal both rank #1). Never promise a number pre-quote. Size the opportunity, source it, attach it to a process, refuse to promise a figure. The refusal builds credibility. Banned phrases in auto-copy: "will save", "guarantee", "we can reduce", "you are overpaying", any bare $/% savings. Mandatory estimate-only disclaimer on every pre-quote proposal.
  2. Number provenance (no hallucinated figures). The LLM never emits a number: it gets a pre-computed facts dict and writes prose around injected, source-tagged values ({value, source}); a post-generation validator regexes every numeral and rejects any not in the allowed-facts set; each figure footnotes its origin ("Form 5500, 2023 filing"). Reuse the api/drafting.py template-first, Claude-optional pattern.
  3. Date every figure ("as of the 2023 filing"). 5500 lags 12-24 months; frame as "what the public record shows," never as current truth. Verify-before-send.
  4. Human checkpoint is the moat, not friction. Never auto-send. Renata generates the insight + scaffold; the broker confirms it is not stale and not politically radioactive (the incumbent might be the owner's brother-in-law), adds one line of relationship context, and signs. A pre-send verification checklist with a logged producer attestation is the broker's E&O defense. The broker owns 100% of the risk; "the software wrote it" is not a defense.
  5. Competitive framing stays account-centric and factual. Name the incumbent only as a neutral public fact if at all; never characterize their competence/ethics. Route the switch through a voluntary AOR letter at renewal.

The CTA / offer ladder

Win the next step, not the sale on paper: (1) read it (the artifact, zero ask) → (2) a 20-min diagnostic call tied to the renewal clock (primary CTA) → (3) permission to market the case (the genius middle rung: low commitment, high value, and it structurally backfills the census/ quotes data Renata lacks, flowing the Artifact-B inputs in) → (4) the AOR letter, after market results prove the gap.

Build verdict & sequence

  • Artifact A → Phase 4.5a (the network layer). Same animal as the Competitor's Book: public-5500 + profile, a conversion driver, zero new compliance surface, riding the existing auth/entitlement seam and drafting.py. The proposal is the easy button escalated from a message to a document. Needs: a broker_profiles table (static sections, onboarding), a benchmarks table (cohort_key, n, p25/p50/p75, source_year), the medical-only Schedule A filter (cost/head currently may blend medical+dental+vision+life; filter to health lines before benchmarking or like does not compare to like), and a document template + provenance validator over drafting.py. No new data sources.
  • Artifact B → Phase 4.5b, behind Gate 2 (census = PII/PHI). Doc-ingest path is a sibling of contacts_upload (owner-scoped, parse → normalize → store): new owner-scoped tables proposal_uploads, plan_designs, census_members, quotes. SBC parse (fixed federal layout + Claude-vision fallback), census column-map, carrier-quote extract with human-verify every number.

Legal: shippable now, two counsel items

The pre-quote public-data proposal is shippable with the estimate-only disclaimer + the mandatory pre-send verification gate. Two items need outside counsel before Artifact B (uploaded data) launches: (1) state insurance-marketing / AOR / replacement rules per operating state; (2) HIPAA business-associate posture and BAA architecture once census/claims (PHI) is uploaded (Renata becomes a subcontractor business associate; BAAs + Security Rule controls required before launch).

Benchmark engine notes

Minimum credible today: median cost/head by employee_band (already in peer_medians()), quoted as a p25-p75 range with the cohort N shown, framed as premium-per-head. Stronger, in order: metro segmentation (companies.metro exists), NAICS (companies.naics exists; watch thin cells with a fallback ladder metro+naics+band → metro+band → state+band → band → global, suppress below a minimum N), then plan-type (needs the medical-only split + funding type).
