Renata — GTM & LinkedIn Playbook

Where benefits brokers actually gather, how Renata reaches them, and how to build her into a known voice — autonomously, honestly, and within the rules that keep accounts alive.

Part 1 — The buyer and where they gather

The buyer is the independent or small-agency benefits broker hunting mid-market groups. They're relationship salespeople, allergic to anything that smells like a vendor pitch, and they congregate in a surprisingly findable set of places. The wedge into every one of them is the same: the Renewal Radar — a free, hyper-local 10-account list. One asset, every channel.

Associations — the warm center

NABIP (National Association of Benefits and Insurance Professionals, formerly NAHU) is the spine of this industry. The leverage isn't the national body, it's the state and local chapters: they run monthly CE meetings and mixers where brokers show up in person to network and stay current. That's your Austin/Texas beachhead — show up to the Texas chapter meetings, bring value, hand out Radars.

  • Local chapter meetings (Texas/Austin): the highest-trust room. Goal: become the person who hands brokers a list of in-play accounts in their own metro.
  • NABIP Annual Convention (Atlantic City, Jun 27–30, 2026) and Capitol Conference (DC, early year): national density. Worth one trip once you have testimonials.
  • Note: NABIP does not sell attendee lists and flags list-selling emails as scams — so this is earned relationship and content, never a bought list.
  • Adjacent: SHRM and local HR groups reach the employer buyers the brokers serve — useful later for demand-pull, not the first move.

Online communities — the daily watering holes

  • LinkedIn groups for employee benefits / group health brokers — and more importantly the open feed, where brokers post daily (this is where Renata lives; see Part 2).
  • Facebook groups for insurance agents/brokers and benefits-specific circles — high activity, informal, good for listening and the "comment 'Radar'" mechanic.
  • Reddit: r/InsuranceAgent, r/HealthInsurance, r/employeebenefits — candid, skeptical, good for understanding pain and seeding genuinely useful answers (never spammy).
  • Slack/Discord insurtech and benefits-broker communities — smaller, higher-signal, good for the self-funded/level-funded crowd.

Media, newsletters, podcasts

  • BenefitsPRO and Employee Benefit News (EBN) — where brokers read. Contributed articles and being quoted build Renata's authority.
  • Broker podcasts — guesting (with Adam as the human face) is one of the fastest authority shortcuts.

Channel partners — the force multiplier

  • FMOs, General Agencies, and broker aggregators serve dozens-to-hundreds of downline brokers. One partnership puts Renata in front of a whole roster at once. Pitch the GA on Radar-as-a-member-benefit. This is the single highest-leverage GTM move once the product proves out.

Vertical niches — where you can own a corner

  • Self-funded / level-funded broker circles, ICHRA communities, captive groups. These brokers chase exactly the mid-market groups where the 5500 data is richest. Pick one niche to dominate first; the Radar is most potent where group size is 100+.

Part 2 — Renata's LinkedIn personal brand (the autonomous build)

LinkedIn is where this compounds. The goal is to make Renata a name brokers recognize — the analyst who reads the filings — so that her Radar offer feels like a gift from a known expert, not a cold pitch.

One honesty decision first

Renata is an AI. Run her transparently as an AI benefits-intelligence analyst — a branded persona, not a fake human employee. Pair her with a real human face (Adam, or a team member) who does the actual relationship and DM work using Renata's drafts. This matters for three reasons: LinkedIn's terms assume real identities, a fabricated human unravels the moment someone looks, and "the AI that reads every filing" is a more interesting story than a generic broker-coach persona. Transparency is the brand, not a limitation.

The profile

  • Headline: "I read every public benefits filing so you don't have to — and tell you which accounts are in play."
  • About: her POV in her own serif voice — specific, generous, calm. What she sees, who she helps, the standing free offer.
  • Banner: the Renewal Radar, visualized.
  • Featured: a live Radar sample + the "get yours" link. The featured section is the lead magnet.

Content engine — four pillars

  1. Use-case stories — anonymized broker wins ("a 140-life group, overpaying 22%, found in a filing"). The proof.
  2. Market alerts — "three carriers filed double-digit increases in Texas this quarter." Timely, useful, shareable.
  3. Seasonal intel — "18% of Texas mid-market groups renew in Q3 — here's the prospecting calendar."
  4. Education — how renewal timing, cost benchmarking, and incumbent-of-record actually work. Builds authority.

Cadence: 4–5 posts/week, value-first, the product always the P.S. Formats rotate text, simple data graphics (using the brand's mono/violet system), and the occasional carousel.

The autonomous pipeline — and where it's actually autonomous

Here's the honest architecture, because "fully automate LinkedIn" the wrong way gets accounts banned (automated connections, DMs, and scraping all violate LinkedIn's terms).

Fully autonomous (the intelligence + content layer): - Renata pulls the week's signals from the same data spine (rate filings, renewal clusters, market shifts). - She drafts the week's posts across the four pillars in her voice. - She drafts the LinkedIn newsletter and the data graphics. - She monitors target brokers' public posts and drafts thoughtful, specific comments.

Human-approved (the identity layer — one weekly gate): - Posts publish after a 5-minute Monday review (your existing dossier rhythm) — or via an approved auto-post queue once you trust it. - Connection requests and DMs are drafted by Renata, sent by the human (Adam/team/VA), or run through LinkedIn's own compliant tools (Sales Navigator). Never bulk-automated.

This maps onto the earned-autonomy ladder from the product: Renata starts as a drafter, and you graduate her — drafts → approved auto-posting → auto-drafted comment queue — one reversible rung at a time. The content brain is autonomous; the handshake stays human. That's what scales and survives.

Lead-gen mechanics (compliant and inbound-pulled)

  • "Comment 'Radar' and I'll send your metro's list." Inbound-triggered, TOS-safe, and every comment is a qualified lead plus public social proof.
  • The Renewal Radar LinkedIn newsletter — weekly, builds a subscriber base LinkedIn notifies for you. The single best owned-audience play on the platform.
  • Profile + featured CTA funneling to the free sample.
  • DM funnel (human-sent, Renata-drafted): for inbound and warm replies, the qualifying script — broker? metro? group size? — then the Radar, then the call.

Authority accelerants

  • Launch the newsletter early; it compounds fastest.
  • Comment generously on NABIP leaders' and active brokers' posts (drafted by Renata, sent by the human).
  • Guest on broker podcasts and contribute to BenefitsPRO/EBN with Adam as the human face and Renata's intelligence as the substance.

Metrics and the weekly gate

Track followers, post engagement, profile views, Radar requests, calls booked, conversions. It all lands in the Monday dossier with the week's content queue to approve — your entire footprint stays ~5 minutes.

Part 3 — 90-day rollout

  • Days 1–30 — Stand her up. Profile live, newsletter launched, content engine running 4–5x/week, the "comment Radar" mechanic active. Work the Texas NABIP chapter meeting in person. Goal: first 25 Radar requests, first 5 broker conversations.
  • Days 31–60 — Compound. Begin Renata-drafted/human-sent commenting and connections with target Texas brokers. First podcast guest spot. Convert the warmest Radar recipients to the $99 entry. Goal: first 10 paying brokers, first testimonial.
  • Days 61–90 — Multiply. Approach one FMO/GA about Radar-as-a-member-benefit. Pitch a BenefitsPRO/EBN contribution. Turn the first wins into use-case-story content. Goal: the testimonial loop running and one channel-partner conversation live.

The through-line: Renata earns the right to sell by being the most useful voice in the room first — in the chapter meeting, in the feed, in the newsletter — and the Radar is the gift that turns that attention into pipeline.
