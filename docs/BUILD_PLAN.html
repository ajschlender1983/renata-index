Renata — Build & Operating Plan

The renewal oracle for employee-benefits brokers. An always-on machine that finds accounts in play, explains why, and drafts the outreach — with a named AI expert as its face.

1. The name

Product + avatar share one name: Renata.

Latin renata = "reborn / renewed." It literally means renewal, and it's a natural, credible human first name — so the same word works as a software brand and as a subject-matter-expert persona who posts on LinkedIn, runs the chatbot, and signs the emails. Nickname "Rena" for casual touchpoints.

  • Tagline: "Renata knows when your groups are in play."
  • One-liner: Renata reads every public benefits filing so brokers don't have to — and tells you which accounts are about to switch.

Two alternates if you want to swap (this is Gate 1, below): Vera (Latin "truth," clean oracle angle) or Iris (the eye that sees what's coming). My strong pick is Renata — the renewal etymology is too good to leave on the table.

2. The operating model — three lanes

This is the heart of the ask: a system that runs itself, that I produce and manage, with only small occasional gates for you.

Lane 1 — The Machine (runs 24/7, zero humans)

Ingests data, refreshes it, scores every company, generates territory lists, drafts outreach, publishes queued content, and triages inbound chat. Never sleeps, never asks permission within its lane.

Lane 2 — The Manager (me — I produce & maintain)

I write and maintain the pipelines, tune the scoring, write Renata's content and sequences, watch system health, assemble your reports, and surface the few decisions that actually need you. When something breaks or a number moves, I fix or flag it — you don't operate the machine, you receive it.

Lane 3 — The Owner (you — occasional gates only)

You make the handful of high-leverage calls. Everything else is delegated. The gates are deliberately few and mostly one-time:

# Gate When Why it needs you
1 Name + brand voice Now (one-time) Renata vs Vera vs Iris; the persona's tone
2 Compliance green-light Before go-live (one-time) Sign-off on data-use and outreach posture
3 Pricing + offer lock Before go-live (revisitable) $/mo, free-sample terms
4 Spend approvals As needed You make all purchase/investment calls — paid data, ads, tools
5 "Publish as me?" Per identity Anything going out under your real name/LinkedIn vs Renata's
6 Weekly green-light Mondays, ~5 min Approve the week's outbound batch + content queue

Reporting rhythm

A Monday-noon dossier (fits your existing money-date cadence) lands each week: accounts added, lists shipped, replies and conversions, system health, and the one or two decisions waiting on you — each as a single tap (Approve / Hold / Edit). That's the entire weekly footprint for you.

3. The data spine

Every source is public and free. One is the foundation; the rest are the trigger layers that turn a list into a prediction.

Source What it gives Role Access note
DOL Form 5500 (EFAST2 datasets) Renewal/plan-year month, incumbent broker, carrier, participant count, cost per head Foundation — the static picture Annual bulk + monthly deltas; lags up to ~9.5 mo (why triggers matter)
State WARN notices Layoffs / headcount shocks Trigger — plan disruption Per-state portals; scrape + normalize
SEC Form D (EDGAR API) Recent private raises Trigger — growth / new capacity Clean public API
Job-posting velocity Hiring surges = outgrowing plan Trigger — expansion Provider or scrape; lighter-weight signal
State DOI rate filings (SERFF) Carrier just filed a big increase Trigger — strongest "in play" signal Public access varies by state — start with the open ones, don't overpromise nationwide

The honest constraints: 5500 detail is richest for plans with 100+ participants, so the sweet spot is brokers hunting mid-market groups; and the filing lag is real — which is exactly why the fresh trigger layers are the moat, not a nice-to-have.

4. The MVP build — phased

Roughly a 3–4 week build in your Claude/Cursor style. Each phase ends in something demoable.

Phase 0 — Foundation (days 1–3)

  • Stand up infra: Supabase (Postgres + auth + edge functions) and a Next.js + Tailwind front end.
  • Resolve Gate 1 (name) and Gate 2 (compliance posture) so nothing downstream gets rebuilt.
  • Land the first 5500 dataset, raw.

Phase 1 — The Oracle (days 3–10)

  • Ingest + parse 5500 into the schema (below).
  • Compute two things per company: renewal-month and cost-vs-peer benchmark (flag "paying 22% above metro median for group size").
  • Ship the broker-territory dashboard: ranked accounts for a broker's metro + group-size filter.
  • Build the free-sample generator — the same pipeline outputs a 10-account teaser. This is both the lead magnet and the demo.

Phase 2 — The Easy Button (days 10–16)

  • Claude API drafts outreach per account, citing the renewal month, the cost benchmark, and the incumbent by name.
  • Renata in-app: explains why each account is flagged in plain English (reason codes → sentences).
  • Stripe for $/mo. Self-serve from the free sample.

Phase 3 — Trigger fusion (days 16–24)

  • Add WARN, Form D, job velocity, and rate filings (open states first).
  • Compute the In-Play Score (below). Now the dashboard ranks by switch likelihood, not just renewal date — the thing nobody sells at indie-broker prices.

Phase 4 — Renata goes public (parallel, from day 10)

  • LinkedIn persona live; content engine publishing weekly.
  • "Ask Renata" site chatbot capturing and qualifying leads.

Phase 5 — Autonomous loop (day 24+)

  • The machine runs end-to-end; Monday dossiers begin; gates settle into rhythm.

6. The In-Play Score (v1 — transparent & tunable)

Rules-based on purpose: every point maps to a reason code Renata can speak aloud. No black box.

Signal Weight Reason code
Renewal in next 90 days High "Renews in "
Cost ≥ X% above peer benchmark High "Overpaying vs metro peers"
Carrier rate increase filed High "Carrier just raised rates"
WARN / headcount shock Med "Workforce in flux"
Recent funding round Med "Just raised — new capacity"
Hiring surge Med "Outgrowing current plan"

Output: 0–100 score + plain-English reason codes. Tunable weights as real conversion data arrives (I retune; you don't have to).

7. Renata as a person — the avatar

One persona, three jobs. This is what makes it a magical oracle with a face instead of another database.

Character: Renata is an AI benefits-intelligence analyst. She reads every public filing in the country so brokers don't have to. Generous with insight, allergic to fluff, never salesy. She talks in specifics — months, dollars, carriers — not adjectives.

Job 1 — Thought leader (LinkedIn). Weekly posts telling use-case stories: anonymized broker wins ("how one Austin broker found a 140-life group overpaying 30%"), market alerts ("which carriers just filed double-digit increases in TX"), and seasonal intel ("18% of Texas mid-market groups renew in Q3 — here's the list-building playbook"). Value first, always. The product is the P.S., never the headline.

Job 2 — Front door (chatbot). "Ask Renata" on the site and in DMs: qualifies (broker? metro? group size?), hands over a free sample on the spot, books the call. The qualification is the lead gen — every conversation ends with a named, scored sample list.

Job 3 — In-product assistant. Explains each flagged account and drafts the outreach. The same voice the broker met on LinkedIn now works inside their pipeline.

Compliance gate worth naming now: fully automated LinkedIn DMs/scraping violate LinkedIn's TOS and get accounts banned. Renata's outbound runs human-in-the-loop (she drafts, a real account sends) or through compliant channels — that's a feature, not a limitation, and it's covered by Gate 5.

8. Creative direction — sales touchpoints

The lead magnet: "The Renewal Radar." A free, hyper-local one-pager — 10 companies in your metro entering their renewal window in the next 90 days, with estimated spend and incumbent broker. It's free because it's auto-generated from the same pipeline. It sells itself because it's specific.

The one-sentence pitch: "I'll send you 10 companies in your area whose health plan renews in the next 90 days, what they pay, and who their broker is — free. If it's useful, it's $399/mo."

Visual identity: clean, analyst-grade, trustworthy — think "Bloomberg terminal for benefits," not "MarTech SaaS." Restrained palette, real data on every screen, Renata's mark present but understated. (I can generate a Renata portrait + brand kit on your say-so.)

Email sequence (signed by Renata, escalating to you to close): 1. The free Radar list, delivered. 2. "Two of those renew next month — want the outreach drafted?" 3. A market-rate-increase alert for their metro (pure value). 4. Soft close: the upgrade, framed as "see all 60, not just 10." 5. You step in personally for warm closes — the only place your time is spent.

GTM beachhead — Austin first. Work the NABIP Austin chapter, BenefitsPro, and broker LinkedIn/Facebook groups. Your first 10 customers come from you personally handing Texas brokers a Radar list; the testimonial loop ("closed a 120-life group off the first list") compounds from there.
