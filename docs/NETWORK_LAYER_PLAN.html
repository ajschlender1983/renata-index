Renata — Network / Directory Layer

The native network layer: a living directory of employers, incumbent brokers, and peer brokers (with a "Renata member" badge), plus "Connect my network" as the conversion trigger. Produced via a four-expert strategic-resolve session (PM/UX, Growth, CTO, Legal) on 2026-06-13. This is the design and build-order of record for the feature.

The core idea

Renata already knows the map. Every 5500 Schedule A names the employer, the carrier, and the broker of record. The score already says which accounts are in play. The directory turns that latent map into a screen brokers can walk: their market, their competitors' books, and their own people, with one button ("Connect my network") that converts.

Decisions locked

Paywall placement: Option B (connect free, pay to unlock the intelligence)

Connecting your network is free and is the activation event. The paywall is the recommendation reveal: scores, reason codes, competitive intelligence, and warm-intro drafts. Growth and CTO both landed here decisively, and it matches the founder's own lean.

Why B over A ("connect triggers paywall"): - The graph fills from free users, so paid value compounds. Gating "connect" throttles the network-effect flywheel at its source. - The wall lands at peak desire (seeing which of your accounts are in play and what to send), not on a chore (connecting). - It reuses the EXISTING entitlement() masking projection from the serving API. Warm-path COUNT is the masked free view; warm-path IDENTITY is the paid unmasked view. Zero new gating machinery, one place in the code that knows what is paid.

The free / paid line:

Tier Features
Free forever (fuels the loop + FOMO) Masked Radar, directory browse, member badges + "who's in my metro" counts, Connect my network, the incumbent map (who holds what, from 5500)
Paywall (the act-on-it value) In-play scores + reason codes, competitive incumbent intelligence ("Competitor's Book"), warm-intro drafting + outreach
Post-paid upsell Defensive radar ("who's hunting your book")

Trigger: the upgrade prompt fires on the recommendation-reveal click, never on connect or browse. Copy names the specific threat and ties to the annual price.

The LinkedIn question: no scraping. Three compliant doors instead.

Crawling/scraping LinkedIn profiles is RED — do not build it. It breaches LinkedIn's User Agreement (hiQ v. LinkedIn actually lost on breach of contract; "public to read" defeats the CFAA but not the TOS), risks account bans, and the use of scraped data is prohibited even when access is public. This is a build-time prohibition: no headless browsers against LinkedIn, no "just the public ones," no buying scraped datasets.

"Connect my network" is sourced from data the user owns or authorizes: 1. Sign in with LinkedIn (OIDC) — identity only (name, email, photo). Returns NO connections (that API is deprecated). Use for frictionless signup + broker-identity verification, not network import. 2. User-uploaded LinkedIn export — the user downloads their own Connections.csv from LinkedIn (their data-portability right) and uploads it. Compliant when the user does the export/upload themselves, attests they have the right to share, and the data is purpose-limited to that user's own view (never merged into the saleable directory). 3. Email / CRM contact import — OAuth or CSV from the broker's own book. Most defensible. Make this the default door; LinkedIn export is the power-user add-on.

The directory of NON-member brokers is built from public 5500 incumbent-broker data, not scraping. Showing "broker of record per 2023 Form 5500" is a public-business-record directory (green). The line: public-record facts are fine; a profile enriched with LinkedIn-derived photo/bio/headline is not. Non-member entries get a "claim or remove this listing" link.

The killer feature: "Competitor's Book"

Show a broker which accounts their named competitor (the incumbent of record) currently holds that are now in play. All public 5500 data, legally green. Framing is the only lever: keep copy account-centric ("compete for this renewal; current broker of record: [Name] per 2023 filing"), never predatory ("steal [Name]'s clients") — predatory framing manufactures tortious-interference/defamation optics that the neutral facts otherwise avoid.

The wow amplifier: warm-intro paths

The network's job is to make the existing in-app easy button warmer. When the user has connected their network, the account view shows the path ("You -> Dana Reyes -> Marcus Lin, CFO") and Renata drafts a warm intro plus a second action, "Ask Dana to intro first" (the note to the mutual connection asking for the forward). Same human-sent "Send as me" model. On the free tier the warm-intro button shows greyed: "Warm intros unlock when you connect your network" — the wow is visible before it is paid for.

The growth engine: the two-sided member badge

The badge (a small "member" mark) signals that a competitor uses the same intelligence you do, which reframes Renata from "a tool I bought" to "table stakes in my market." The K-factor lever: when a connected broker tracks a non-member incumbent's accounts, that incumbent gets a defensive FOMO notification ("a broker in your metro is tracking 2 of your accounts as in-play") and joins to defend, becoming the next connected broker. Targeted, self-sending, compounding.

Social-proof rule (enforced): aggregate and ambient, never targeted and behavioral. Allowed: "14 brokers on Renata in Austin metro", "3 brokers in your metro joined in the last 30 days." Banned: "Capitol Risk viewed Hill Country 4 times", any presence/last-seen/activity about a named broker.

Member-on-member book visibility (LOCKED 2026-06-13)

Decision: mask members' own clients. A member's current clients are hidden from other members' Competitor's Book detail — other members see aggregate counts ("holds 34 accounts, 5 in play") but not the client list. This protects members from being hunted by peers, reduces churn, and is the directory-side twin of the existing "never auto-contact existing clients" guardrail. Non-member (public 5500) broker books remain fully visible.

Architecture summary (full DDL in the CTO consult)

One canonical identity table is the key decision: do not split "broker" and "member." A member is a broker_identities row whose user_id is non-null. New tables:

  • broker_identities — canonical broker (member iff user_id IS NOT NULL), with badge, norm_person/norm_agency/npn keys.
  • broker_mentions — append-only raw plans.incumbent_broker strings (loader write path untouched), idempotent on (plan_id, raw_name, source_year).
  • broker_match — the single source of truth for resolution: confidence, method, and a state machine (auto / pending_review / confirmed / rejected).
  • broker_account_rel — materialized employer<->incumbent-broker edge for the directory, carrying source_year so "incumbent as of 2023 filing" is shown honestly.
  • contacts_upload + contacts — the user's uploaded network, namespaced by owner_user_id, with stored consent text and one-click batch purge.
  • contact_match + company_contacts + warm_paths — resolved network edges and the precomputed per-user warm paths the Radar badges read.

Entity resolution is deterministic (no ML): normalize -> block on shared keys -> additive confidence score -> state machine (>=0.85 auto, 0.55-0.85 human-review, <0.55 new provisional identity; NPN is the gold auto-confirm key). Member signup runs the same scorer at a higher bar with an "Is this you?" self-claim. A merge_identities(keep, drop) primitive makes mistakes cheap to fix.

Warm-intro graph is two indexed Postgres joins (no graph DB): warm intro is 1st or 2nd degree only, routed through Renata members. Precomputed into warm_paths so the Radar can badge "you have a way in" instantly.

Contact ingest is NOT a SourceAgent (uploads are user-triggered, per-user, PII-bearing, never company_id-keyed Signals). Build a parallel ContactIngestor (parse -> normalize -> resolve) that heartbeats into source_health under contacts:{upload_id} so observability still flows.

Sequencing: new Phase 4.5 (pulled out of Phase 6 CRM)

The network layer is a conversion driver; CRM sync is a post-sale retention feature. Split them. Insert Phase 4.5 — Network Layer after Stripe (Phase 4, needs auth+billing to be the conversion trigger) and before live DOL ingest (Phase 5) / CRM (Phase 6).

MVP cut line: everything buildable from 5500 data already owned, zero new compliance surface.

Ships first (Phase 4.5a): 1. Brokers tab (incumbent + peer brokers from incumbent_broker). 2. "Held by {broker}" on every account row. 3. Competitor's Book view (a broker's accounts filtered + sorted by score). 4. Member badges + metro member counts. 5. "Connect my network" as a LOCKED, instrumented teaser with a blurred warm-intro example (validates demand before the hard integration is built; measure tap rate first).

Comes second (Phase 4.5b, gated on demand + Gate 2 sign-off): 6. The contact matcher (CSV/email first, LinkedIn export when the flow is ready). 7. Warm-intro drafts + "Ask Dana to intro first." 8. The relationship Map view (only once enough edges exist to make it dense). 9. Member-to-member referral nudges + the book-masking rules.

Top risks

  1. Entity-resolution accuracy (make-or-break): a false warm path kills credibility. Conservative auto-merge (0.85), human-review band, honest degree/source display, cheap merge primitive.
  2. Uploaded contacts leaking into the saleable directory: hard data silo by owner_user_id; uploads serve only the uploading user, never merged or sold.
  3. Enriching non-member entries with scraped/social data: public-record facts only.
  4. Predatory competitive-intel framing: account-centric copy only.
  5. Empty-network cold start: the 5500 incumbent map pre-populates every metro, so the graph is never empty; members are a badged overlay on a public base.

Compliance (Gate 2 additions)

Two items need outside counsel before go-live: (a) final TOS / Privacy Policy / upload clauses review (the data-silo commitment, third-party deletion, TDPSA/CCPA fit) — this is the Gate 2 sign-off, triggered before the first uploaded network or first paying broker; and (b) only IF paid third-party data enrichment is later added. The feature ships compliantly on public 5500 records + user-owned uploads with zero third-party enrichment, so (b) never blocks v1. Member directory listing needs in-flow signup notice + self-serve opt-out.
