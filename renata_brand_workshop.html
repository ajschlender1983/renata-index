The mark is Renata in the room.

A study in subtraction. Ten ways to simplify the Aperture for a premium professional-services brand, plus the logic that lets the logo behave like a personalized agent assistant whose states speak quietly to the user.

01

The through-line

Before we simplify, we name what cannot be simplified away. Every direction below is a different sentence built from the same two words.

One calm curve, drawn toward one lit point of attention.
The arc is the analyst's discipline: the patient reading of a whole field. The violet point is Renata herself, the one account she surfaced for you. Premium is the act of removing everything that is not those two things. Never simplify out the curve attending to the point.
02

Ten simplifications

From three concentric arcs down to one, to none, to a single letterform. Each holds the through-line. Each is built to survive at 16 pixels. The small chip on every card is the mark at favicon scale.

The assistant, in six states

This is what makes the logo a presence rather than a parked badge. The arc never moves and never changes color. Only the point speaks. The discipline holds steady; Renata reacts.

She is here, not working. The point settles to ink. Nothing moves.

Listening

She is taking something in. The point warms to violet and breathes once.

Scanning
She is reading the field. The point pulses; a violet trace orbits the arc.
point · pulse + orbit
Found
Insight
She surfaced an account. The point resolves to teal and blooms once. The signature moment.
point · teal bloom
Timing
Renewal clock
A window is opening. The point shifts amber and breathes slowly.
point · amber breathe
Alert
At risk
Something needs you. The point goes to alert and pulses once, firmly. Premium stays calm.
point · single firm pulse
Color carries meaning
The arc is always ink. Only the point changes color. Violet is presence, teal is opportunity, amber is timing, alert is risk. One language, learned in a glance.
Motion carries liveness
One variable changes per state. Cycles run slow, 1.4 to 2.4 seconds, always eased, never linear. Restraint in motion is what reads as premium.
Reduced motion is whole
Every state has a static end-frame. With motion off, the mark still tells the truth through color alone. Nothing depends on animation.
04

Where the mark lives

Placement is tied to state. The mark is not chrome in a corner; it is Renata, present, and its behavior changes with what she is doing.

App bar · the always-there assistant

28–32px, left-aligned, optically centered. This instance carries the live state: it is her heartbeat across the app.
Renata Market warm ME

Inline · when she speaks

20px, point-forward, at the optical baseline of her first line. It pulses while she writes, then resolves teal if she found something.
I pulled Hill Country Health from their filing. They are overpaying about 22%, and Aetna just filed an increase, so this is a strong week to reach out.

Thinking · the brand is the loader

Never a generic spinner. The arc's own trace sweeps the pulsing point. Renata's geometry does the waiting.
Reading the latest filings…

Empty state · waiting, not absent

40–56px, centered, one slow breath every few seconds. The mark does the emotional work; the copy stays sparse.
Nothing needs you right now.

Favicon · 16px, on any tab

Ship the mass-based marks here. The Eclipse never breaks up on a light or dark tab; halos are dropped and the point is thickened.

The lockup

Mark plus the Newsreader wordmark, sentence case. The point sits on the side facing the name and hands the eye into it.
Renata
Gap to wordmark: 0.45 × cap-height
Clear space: 0.75 × mark height, all sides
Align: mark center to x-height center
Below 20px: drop the wordmark

Do

  • Let only the point carry state. Color lives in the point; the arc is immovable ink.
  • Animate with ease, never linear and never fast. Calm is the premium signal.
  • Reserve the teal bloom for a genuine found opportunity, so it stays meaningful.

Don't

  • Recolor or gradient the arc. It breaks the discipline-and-presence split.
  • Keep the halo at 16px or below. Halos turn to mud; thicken the point instead.
  • Let the mark sit static while she is working. A still logo reads as dead, not live.
