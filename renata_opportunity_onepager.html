Why now — the AI window

This data has always been public. What changed is the cost of using it. Cleaning messy filings, fusing a dozen fragmented sources, and writing tailored outreach used to require an enterprise data team — which is exactly why only a few incumbents could do it, and only at enterprise prices. AI collapsed that cost to near zero.

  • AI does the unglamorous work — normalizing dirty 5500 data, reconciling sources, drafting per-account outreach — at the marginal cost of a model call.
  • A small AI-native team ships what took an enterprise. The working data spine, scoring, and Radar already exist, built in weeks, not years.
  • Speed is the moat-for-now. The window is the gap between "AI makes this cheap" and "incumbents retrofit AI onto legacy databases." We move first and own the indie segment they ignore.
  • Agentic, not a tool. The leap isn't a better search box — it's an agent that finds, scores, and acts. Incumbents sell data; we sell the outcome.
