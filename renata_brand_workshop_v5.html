Brand Workshop V · Shaping the R

From assembled parts to a drawn letter.

You are right: the pick still reads as a circle, a rounded line, and a rounded rectangle. These variations keep the solid mass and the aperture, but reshape the R as a crafted letterform: tapered legs with cut terminals, shaped stems, and bowls that breathe off the perfect circle.

The starting point. A circle bowl, a round-capped line for the leg, and a rounded rectangle for the stem. Three primitives sitting next to each other. Below, the same idea drawn as one letter.
01

Six shaping studies

Same colour, same aperture, same weight. Only the curvature and the terminals change. The leg now tapers and cuts rather than ending in a ball; the stem and bowl carry a little type-designer's tension.

16px
ACut Terminals
The smallest move: the leg becomes a tapered blade with a flat-cut foot, the stem squares off. The ball-ends are gone.
16px
BFlared Foot
The stem widens slightly toward the base and the bowl flattens a touch. A grounded, monumental stance, like a cut serif without the serifs.
16px
CVertical Bowl
The bowl is taller than it is wide, giving the R a humanist vertical stress. It stops reading as a logo circle and starts reading as a letter.
16px
DCalligraphic
The leg sweeps out of the bowl as a curved, tapering stroke with an angled tip, and the stem carries a faint entasis. The most drawn-by-hand of the set.
16px
EContinuous
The leg flows out of the bowl as one unbroken curve rather than a struck-on line, so the eye reads a single gesture from stem to tail.
16px
FCut Terminals · reversed
The cleanest study on the ink ground, with the aperture cut to the dark. This is how it lives in an app bar or on a dark deck.
Renata. Drawn, not assembled.
